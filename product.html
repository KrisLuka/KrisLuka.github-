The start of the Apple Watch The Apple Watch started being designed in Apple's headquarters in California, where after enough working prototypes the Apple Watch Series X was released, this was, in other words, a public prototype of the Apple Watch. A fun fact about the Apple Watch: They named it the Apple Watch instead of the IWatch because the name IWatch was already trademarked, as companies started trying to trademark names that apple would want to use in their standard I (followed by the product name) template, just so these companies could earn money by sueing Apple. It took some time after the release for it to attract attention, initially only reeling in loyal Apple fans but slowly started to intrigue more and more users, including people less dedicated to the brand.

The problems the Apple Watch solves We all know about FitBits, they're not new to the market, they work great for athletes, but that's the problem, it's intended to be used by athletes, not really the average Joe. That's where the Apple Watch comes in, boasting most if not all features the FitBits had while also adding convenience and a wider appeal.
